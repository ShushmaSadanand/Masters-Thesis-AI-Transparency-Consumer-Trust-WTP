## Research Paradigm & Theoretical Foundation
**File:** `1. Initial Methodology & Literature review.pdf` (Active Research Protocol & Literature Synthesis)

As e-commerce platforms increasingly shift pricing and matching operations to black-box machine learning algorithms, a critical gap opens between technological efficiency and consumer psychological comfort. This thesis investigates a central problem: *Does lifting the veil on how an AI recommendation engine functions build transactional trust, or does a complex explanation trigger cognitive overload and diminish a consumer's financial Willingness to Pay (WTP)?*

### Academic & Experimental Architecture

#### 1. The XAI Transparency-Trust Matrix
* **Mechanisms of Explanation:** Constructs a cross-sectional matrix comparing different levels of **Explainable AI (XAI)** presentation styles (e.g., Feature-Importance Badges vs. Natural Language Counterfactuals vs. Baseline Zero-Explanation controls).
* **Latent Trust Construction:** Synthesizes historical behavioral literature to map how user-perceived *benevolence, ability, and integrity* react when an algorithm transparently shows its parameters.

#### 2. Experimental Design & Controlled Intervention
* **Scenario-Based Simulation Pipeline:** To test this empirically, the project builds a simulated digital storefront environment. Users interact with AI recommendation outputs under randomized transparency constraints.
* **The Willingness to Pay (WTP) Vector:** Rather than asking simple satisfaction survey questions, the framework evaluates financial intent using specialized econometric instruments (e.g., the **Becker-DeGroot-Marschak (BDM) mechanism** or contingent item valuation sliders) to capture real economic trade-offs.

#### 3. Planned Analytical Modeling
* **Structural Equation Modeling (SEM):** Once the data gathering pipeline goes live, the primary analysis will utilize **Covariance-Based SEM (CB-SEM)** to unpack direct and indirect paths between transparency triggers (independent variables), perceived accountability (mediator), and conversion choice thresholds/WTP (dependent variables).
